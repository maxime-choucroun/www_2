Process Description


OBJECTIVES

  • Maintain the accuracy of the book to actual inventory quantities
  • Identify differences between physical stock and stock on hand in the system and update the system
  • Investigate differences and close any gaps in other operational processes that cause the shrinkage
  • Notify the relevant managers of their shrinkage figures and to highlight various problem areas within the various material categories
  • Have a physical inventory process that is carried out on a regular basis (to avoid any distorsion in the other operations)



PROCESS STEP SUMMARY
    1) Physical inventory methods
    • Criteria for physical inventory methods and frequency determination
        1. World Business Unit
        2. Type of materials (Raws & Packs, finished products)
        3. Type of storage (Racks/main storage, Picking zones, "on-line")

    • Physical inventory methods
        1. Full physical inventory
            1. All materials counted physically at a certain date
            2. Counting can be organised by :
                  1. family of products
                  2. storage areas

        2. Cycle counting
            1. Inventory counted at regular intervals
            2. Cycle counting based on the materials ABC classification
            3. ABC classification :
                  1. Based on :
                        1. Stock turns
                        2. Value
                        3. Family of product (family of raws or type of products in picking)
                        4. Replenishment leadtime
                  2. Determined :
                        1. Automatically
                              At the end of the fiscal year, automatic calculation program is used.
                              Criteria used by the program : stock turns and value
                        2. Manually
                              Classification can be changed manually during the fiscal year, taking into account :
                                  - family of product
                                  - replenishment leadtime
                  3. Checked and set by Logistics people
            4. Inventory counting frequency defined by ABC classification
                  The more materials are critical (eg. the higher turnover is), the higher the cycle counting frequency is

        3. Full/Empty strategy
            1. In warehouse with bins management
            2. Physical inventory performed at the "full/empty" level
            3. In case of discrepancies, identification of the bin, pallet and product
        4. Physical zone-based counting
            1. In warehouse with bins management
            2. Materials counted at different periods based on their physical position (storage area/aisles/rows/columns)
            3. Frequency of each inventory area defined by Warehouse Manager



      2) Physical inventory steps

      • Determine the physical inventory method to use and the products/area/class to count
      • Prepare inventory :
            1. Organise the counting process
            2. Print the work list
                  1. Blind physical inventory (without quantity)
                  2. Two exceptions : Full/Empty counting + Quick inventory of the main storage
            3. Block inventory/selected areas for goods movements
            4. Freeze inventory
                  Not to impact the inventory relevant to the physical inventory by materials movement and to have the right references for the differences posting
            5. Dispatch the work based on physical organisation
      • Count inventory
            1. Data to check depending on the storage categories:
                  1. Quantity
                  2. Pallet number
                  3. Material number
                  4. Number of boxes
                  5. Number of units
                  6. DLC or DLUO
            2. Recount if necessary
            3. Print new label if old label doesn't fit the content
            4. Barcode scanning/radio frequency used during physical counting
      • Enter count results and analyse results/differences
      • Post differences
            1. Validation, depending on the differences value, by :
                  1. Warehouse staff
                  2. Logistics Manager
                  3. Finance
            2. Differences posting tolerances defined at user level to manage validation authorisation


    BUSINESS PROCESS DESCRIPTION


    Physical inventories are required to ensure that the inventory quantities and values in the system are accurate.
    The physical inventory of the warehouse must be carried out at least once per fiscal year to balance its inventory.
    It allows then to reduce the surprise "Out of stock" conditions that can :
        1. Prevent Manufacturing operations
        2. Prevent shipping
        3. Create internal delay (search for materials...)

    The physical inventory process described below can be used in :
        1. Factories
        2. Distribution centers


    The materials concerned by physical inventory are :
        1. Raws & Packs
        2. Finished products
        3. Semi-finished products
              Remark : the physical inventory for Semi-finished products is the same as for Raws & Packs and finished products because they are stored the same way.

    The process above describes how to perform a physical inventory when :
        1. There is no bin management, only a general big location (generally for raws and packs)
        2. There is bin management within the warehouse

            1-1) Guiding principles

    • 2 physical inventory methods will be used :
          1. Full physical counting
                All materials must be physically counted at a certain date (eg. the end of the fiscal year)
                The full physical counting may be limited to a family of materials (in particular for raws materials) in order to allow the repartition of the counting activity all along an inventory cycle (the week for example).
                The full physical counting may be limited by area (zone) in the warehouse.
                During the counting, the entire warehouse (zone) must be blocked for materials movement.
          2. Cycle counting
                Inventory is counted at regular intervals within the fiscal year.
                All materials will be classified as A, B, C based upon:
                    • stock turnover
                    • value
                    • family of product
                    • replenishment lead time (to be confirmed)
                The frequency of the counting for each class is then determined.
                The higher the turnover is, the more frequently the material is counted because possibility of stock's differences is higher when turnover is higher.

                Inventory counting frequencies proposal :

                Product classification
                Inventory Turn
                Inventory freguency
                A
                The biggest
                every month
                B
                Midle
                every 3 month
                C
                Low
                every 6 month

                Option "manual classification" : ABC classification may be defined manually, for example :
                - to identify 2 types of products in picking
                - to identify several families of raws materials.
                - based on value, quality/traceability requirements
                => The user could then manually choice a class, and ask for it's inventory.

                Advantages
                    • This strategy allows to have correct stock level without regulary organizing "Full physical inventory" .

                Disadvantages
                    • All the storage positions are not controlled (eg : empty bins or some storage area). The warehouse clerk may not identify the discrepancy if there is a pallet in an storage bin which is supposed to be empty.
                    • This strategy can block activities such as picking, because the class selected for the counting (and therefore blocked for movements) may be stored in different areas
          Responsibility
                    • The Logistics Team will be responsible for maintaining the ABC classification
                    • Manual and automatic ABC classification analysis will be used :
                        At the end of the fiscal year, the automatic calculation program is run
                        During the year, the classification can be changed manually
                    • At a material creation, class "A" is set and will be changed by the Logistics Team later on

    • In the case the label doesn't fit the content, another label has to be created with the right information
    • Barcode scanning / radio frequency devices will be used during the physical counting (whenever possible) to avoid any manual treatment, errors and to speed up the process.
    • The inventory count will be organised based on physical organisation.
    • The inventory will be blind (no quantities) with two exceptions:
        • full / empty counting
        • main storage counting for quick verifications
    • For raw materials, the site may use an automatic evaluation of inventory.
      The automatic evaluation memorizes the current stock estimation :
          1. case of materials globally managed in stock (without any specification) : "last inventory + receptions - estimation of consumption (fabrication x components)".
          2. case of containers : every container contains an history of process messages = beginning and end of use (with notification of the used weight); if the last control of weight has not be done for a container, a theoritical one is generated : "last control weight - used weights".
    • For milk inventory, the user notifies the different quantities with the associated rates of fat and protein (in order to evaluate the equivalent quantity of "milk zero", + weights of fat and protein).
      When the site has some white mass still present in the process during the counting (case of dairy products), the quantity of mass is multiplied by the theoritical components, in order to add the equivalent translation (milk zero, fat, protein) to the inventory.
    • In case of external management of warehouse, the inventory description is received via specific interface; this description may be detailed per DLC or DLUO, per pallet ..., according to the expected functions of stock control, tracking of pallets...


        1-2) Guiding principles without bin management

    • In case of stock management by location, the physical inventory is performed with Materials Management (MM) module.
    • The counting is organized by :
          1. storage location
          2. material number
    • The warehouse manager will have to organize the physical inventory work so that all the storage position with the materials planned for counting are actually taken into account in the counting by the warehouse staff

        1-3) Guiding principles when bin management

    • In case of stock management by bin, the physical inventory is performed at the Warehouse Management (WM) level
    • The counting is organized by :
          1. storage position (bin)
          2. eventually by material number
    • Records have to be arranged by bin's position (rows or column). The user should have the possibility for sorting before the physical inventory document printing
    • 2 physical inventory methods will be used (See above "Guiding principles with or without WM")
          1. Full physical counting
          2. Cycle counting

      But for the full physical counting, there are two possible variances : 1 .Full physical inventory (counting)
      All materials are counted at one time.
          1. Full / Empty strategy (strategy of quick control) :
              This strategy uses the same printed physical document as the full physical inventory but the control is done only at the Full / Empty bin level. If during the control the worker finds a bin that is supposed to be empty by the system but is physically occupied, he has to note down the following data to adjust the inventory later on : material number, expiry date, quantity, pallet number, storage bin number
          3. Physical zone- based counting
              The materials are counted at different periods (and not all materials at a certain date) based on their physical position.
              The warehouse can be splitted into different areas. The frequency of each area inventory is then determined by the warehouse manager. Frequency could be different for picking zone and storage area.

      2) Physical inventory steps


      The physical inventory consists in :
          1. Determine the inventory count type (select class or area or product that will be counted)
          2. Maintain the ABC classification regulary if ABC cycle counting is selected
          3. Prepare inventory :
                • Organize the counting process
                • Block selected area
                • Print the work list
                • Dispatch the work based on physical organization
          4. Count Inventory
          5. Enter count results and Analyse results / differences
          6. Post differences


              2-1) Determine the inventory count type

      Depending on the inventory count type initiated, the materials selected for the physical count are different.
      • Cycle counting
      • Full physical count
      Mainly the recommendation is to reduce, when possible, the number of full physical inventory count, to promote cycle counting.
      But full physical count remains the way of work for situations such as :
          - daily inventory for dairy products.
          - regular raw materials inventory per family



              2-2) Maintain the ABC classification if cycle counting

      In the case of a cycle counting, the first step is to initiate an ABC analysis. ABC classification has to be defined beforehand once and for all. Nevertheless, the classes can be maintained afterwards by the Logistics Team who is responsible for the ABC classification maintenance.
      The classification is then used during the inventory prepation :
          • generate cycle count lists
          • count documents
          • schedule the count events.

      • The ABC analysis will be performed either :
          1. Automatically : Program scanning SAP database to classify materials based on Inventory turn
                The automatic calculation will be run once a year.
          2. Manually : manual entry at material level
                During the year, the Logistics team can update manually the classification of a material.

              2-3) Prepare inventory

      Inventory preparation consists in :
        • Organising the counting process
        • Printing the work list, with the following data :
              1. Warehouse and the storage location in which the count is to take place
              2. Bin location (if bin management)
              3. When the count is to take place
              4. Materials to count : material number and description
              5. Quantity (optional)
              6. Unit of measure
              7. Expiry date
              8. Stock status (blocked stock, available stock...)
              9. Batch number (for materials handled in batches)
              10. Special stock to be counted (supplier consignment stock...)
              11. Pallet number (if barcode scanning devices)
        • Dispatching the work based on the physical organisation

      Before starting the inventory count, one more preparation step is to Block or to Freeze the stock to avoid stock movements while counting inventory :
        • Block inventory :
            All stock movements are blocked within the system while the counting or recounting process occurs.
            Once the count results are posted in the system the materials are unblocked.

            When materials are blocked, each time there is a goods movement (eg. goods receipt), there is a error message informing that a physical inventory is being carried out.
        • Freeze inventory :
            If the count result is not posted in the system immediately after the physical count, it can be usefull to just freeze the inventory, so that inventory level relevant to the given physical inventory is not impacted by material movements (authorized in this case) in order to have the right reference for the differences posting.



              2-4) Count inventory

      Inventory count is the main step in the process flow :
          1. Count the inventory
          2. Recount if an error occured (usually a difference between the counted quantity and the system inventory level)
                Based on the count results, a recount may be triggered and a new work list is printed with the materials to be recounted.
      • Barcode inventory count will be used whenever possible to make this process more efficient.
          The scan must occur at the following level :
              1. Pallet label (pallet number and information about pallet)
                    If pallet contains a different quantity from the one announced by the system, the worker must have the possibility to overwrite manually the quantity.
              2. Bulk
                    If the bulk has the materials information on a label, the user scans the material number and adds quantity by
      • Data to check and to notify

      Storage categories
      Data to check and to notify
      Pallets in racks
      		 - Pallet number
      - Quantity on pallet
      Bulk
      		 - Material number
      - Quantity
      Picking zone per pallet
      		 - Pallet number
      - Number of boxes
      Picking zone per carton per DLC or DLUO
      		 - Number of boxes
      - DLC or DLUO
      Picking zone per unit
      		 - Number of units
      - DLC or DLUO
      Milk inventory
      		 - Quantity
      - Fat rate
      - Protein rate

      remark : in case of storage per block (example in water companies), the count may notify a global information = number of pallets per DLUO.
      (because it may be impossible to notice every pallet number in detail).


              2-5) Enter count results and Analyse results / differences


      Once inventory count has been realized :
        • Enter the counted quantities into the system. It must be possible to enter the inventory with a G/L date of the previous month.
        • Compare counted quantities with information system quantities
        • Analyze reasons of mismatch in case of discrepancies



              2-6) Post differences


      Once differences have been investigated and analyzed :
        • Validate differences before posting :
              Depending on warehouses/countries, the valisation can be done by :
                  1. either the warehouse staff
                  2. or by the Logistics manager
                  3. or Finance/Audit
        • Post inventory differences in Accounting
            Tolerances defined at the user level will be used for the posting of stock take differences.
            A user will have a tolerance built in for posting of individual line items as well as the entire document.

            When the differences are posted, the stock quantity is automatically adjusted - reduced or increased- in the system.
            The G/L acccount can be :
                1. either updated straight after the differences posting
                2. or updated at the end of the, the differences being cumulated during the year each time there is an inventory counting.
        • Go Live justification
            A statement must be done to explain how inventory is transfered from previous system to SAP
